My Story

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet consectetur.

  • 1997

    Enter Stage

    I was born in Quezon City, Philippines - the land of jeepneys and mangoes. According to home family videos, I grew up speaking English + Tagalog = Taglish (have evolved to English now but can still understand the mother tongue of my parents well enough to eavesdrop on private conversations.)

  • 2000-2009

    Canadian, Eh?

    My family immigrated to Vancouver when I was 3 years old; we lived in beautiful BC for 5 years where I learned to enjoy playing outside (have kept this trait as an adult) and then 4 years in Calgary where I developed a private taste for country music (again kept mostly private so please don't worry).

  • 2009-2014

    Back Home (?)

    As I entered the tumultous teenage years, my family returned to the Philippines temporarily. Our 5 years there were some of the most challenging and rich years of my life as I learned more about myself and about the world through experiencing life in a developing nation as a young adult.

  • 2015-2019

    La Vie En Montreal

    I spent the last year of high school back in Vancouver (this was the year I first learned and fell in love with programming through a high school elective) and then moved to Montreal, QC to study computer science at McGill University. My time here has been made rich by amazing people, culture, opportunities, and food. I have also learned a thing or two. ;)

  • 2018-Present

    MPCL

    In the summer of 2018, I was hired as web application developer to the Music Perception and Cognition Lab at McGill University. It was largely through my time working for MPCL that I gained an interest in web and software development. It has been so cool to learn and experiment with REST, Django, and the full stack (from database models and front-end HTML).
